Chapter 1. Disorders of the Gastrointestinal Tract

1. Diarrhoea

Definition:
Passage of loose, liquid or watery stools, usually at least three times in a 24-hour period.

Clinical Features:
• Loose/watery stools
• Abdominal cramps
• Nausea or vomiting
• Fever (if infectious)

Treatment:
Non-pharmacological:
• Oral Rehydration Therapy (ORS)
• Continue feeding/breastfeeding
• Zinc supplementation

Pharmacological:
• ORS: 75ml/kg over 4 hours for mild dehydration
• Zinc: 20mg daily for 10-14 days (children)
• Antibiotics NOT routinely recommended

2. Gastroenteritis

Definition:
Inflammation of the gastrointestinal tract involving both stomach and small intestine.

Treatment:
• Rehydration with ORS
• Metronidazole 400mg three times daily (if bacterial)
• Paracetamol 500mg for fever

Chapter 2. Cardiovascular System Disorders

1. Hypertension

Definition:
Persistently raised blood pressure ≥140/90 mmHg

Treatment:
First line:
• Amlodipine 5mg daily
• Hydrochlorothiazide 25mg daily

Second line:
• Lisinopril 10mg daily
• Atenolol 50mg daily

2. Heart Failure

Treatment:
• Furosemide 40mg daily
• Lisinopril 5-10mg daily
• Spironolactone 25mg daily
